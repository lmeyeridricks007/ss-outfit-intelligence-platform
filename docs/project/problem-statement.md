# Problem Statement

## Current problem

SuitSupply customers frequently shop with an anchor product or a specific need in mind, such as a suit, jacket, or occasion, but they still need help assembling the rest of the look. They want answers to practical styling questions:
- what shirt matches this suit
- which tie works with this jacket
- what to wear for an interview, wedding, business meeting, or travel scenario
- how to build a full outfit across multiple categories
- how recommendations should reflect personal style and prior purchases

Current ecommerce recommendation approaches do not reliably solve that problem. They typically emphasize:
- similar products
- frequently bought together suggestions
- popularity or co-occurrence signals

Those approaches can be useful for discovery, but they are insufficient for curated outfit building because they do not consistently reason about compatibility, occasion, weather, season, customer profile, or merchandising intent.

## Who experiences the problem

### Primary affected users
- Online shoppers browsing suits, shirts, shoes, and accessories
- Returning customers with purchase history and recognizable style preferences
- Customers shopping for a defined occasion and needing confidence in what goes together

### Secondary affected users
- In-store stylists who need fast, credible recommendations while advising customers
- Merchandisers who want recommendations to reflect brand standards and current campaigns
- Marketing teams who need recommendation content for email and lifecycle programs
- Product and analytics teams who need measurable recommendation performance and experimentation control

## Why current alternatives are insufficient

- Similar-item recommendations keep customers within one category instead of helping them complete a look.
- Frequently-bought-together logic reflects historical co-occurrence, not present intent or styling quality.
- Generic recommenders rarely distinguish RTW and CM decision-making properly.
- Most systems do not blend AI scoring with curated looks, business rules, and contextual signals in a controlled way.
- Internal style expertise exists but is not structured as a reusable product capability across channels.

## Why now

- SuitSupply already has the merchandising knowledge required to define compatibility and style intent, making the opportunity practical rather than speculative.
- Customers increasingly expect personalization and outfit inspiration, not only product discovery.
- Multi-channel commerce, email, and clienteling workflows need a shared recommendation foundation instead of disconnected channel-specific logic.
- Better recommendation quality directly supports measurable commercial goals such as conversion uplift and larger baskets.

## Consequences of not solving it

- Customers continue to do manual styling work that creates friction and decision fatigue.
- Recommendations remain less relevant, lowering conversion potential and cross-category attachment.
- Internal teams continue to rely on fragmented manual curation and inconsistent channel behavior.
- SuitSupply misses a differentiated opportunity to turn brand styling expertise into a scalable platform capability.

## Core problem to solve

SuitSupply needs a governed outfit intelligence platform that can generate complete-look, context-aware, and personalized recommendations across RTW and CM journeys, while remaining commercially measurable and controllable by merchandising and operational teams.
