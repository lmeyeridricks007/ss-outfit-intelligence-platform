# Vision

## Bootstrap source

- Source issue: GitHub issue #37, "Master product spec: AI Outfit Intelligence Platform (SuitSupply Recommendation Engine)"
- Product name: AI Outfit Intelligence Platform
- Primary brand/use case: SuitSupply recommendation experiences across digital and stylist-assisted channels

## Product vision

Build the recommendation platform that helps SuitSupply customers assemble complete outfits, not just discover isolated items. The platform should combine curated styling knowledge, business rules, customer behavior, and contextual signals so every recommendation surface can suggest a coherent next look for the shopper's intent, occasion, and personal style.

## Why this product should exist

SuitSupply already has strong styling taste, merchandising knowledge, and a product catalog that spans tailored clothing, accessories, and custom options. The current gap is activation: that knowledge is not consistently translated into personalized, data-driven outfit recommendations across ecommerce, email, and in-store workflows. This platform exists to turn styling expertise into a reusable operating capability.

## Long-term ambition

The long-term ambition is to make outfit intelligence a shared decisioning layer across all customer channels:

- ecommerce experiences such as PDP, cart, homepage, and look-builder flows
- lifecycle marketing such as triggered and campaign email
- in-store clienteling and stylist-assisted selling
- RTW and CM recommendation scenarios
- future API-driven mobile or partner surfaces

In its mature form, the platform should act as the source of truth for:

- complete-look recommendations
- cross-sell and upsell logic
- occasion and context-aware styling
- customer-specific personalization
- merchandising controls and campaign overlays
- experimentation and recommendation performance learning

## Differentiators

This product should differentiate itself from typical ecommerce recommendation systems by emphasizing:

1. **Complete-look intelligence**
   - Recommends outfits and style bundles across categories instead of only similar products.
2. **Hybrid recommendation logic**
   - Blends curated looks, compatibility rules, and AI ranking rather than relying on co-occurrence alone.
3. **Context-aware relevance**
   - Uses location, weather, season, occasion, and session context to influence recommendations.
4. **Omnichannel customer understanding**
   - Incorporates online behavior, orders, appointments, store visits, and stylist notes where available.
5. **RTW and CM support**
   - Covers immediate product recommendations and configuration-aware styling guidance for custom garments.
6. **Merchandising governance**
   - Keeps human control over looks, rules, campaigns, and exclusions while enabling personalization.

## Intended market and user impact

For customers, the product should reduce outfit uncertainty and make SuitSupply feel like a trusted style advisor. For internal teams, it should provide a reusable recommendation capability that improves conversion, basket size, and campaign performance without requiring each channel to recreate its own styling logic.

## Strategic outcome

SuitSupply should become better at answering questions like:

- "What completes this suit?"
- "What should I wear for this occasion?"
- "What fits my style and what I already own?"
- "What should we show this customer right now on this channel?"

If successful, the platform becomes a durable competitive advantage: a recommendation system grounded in style compatibility, personalization, and merchandising control rather than commodity product similarity.
