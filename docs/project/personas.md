# Personas

## Persona 1: Style-Seeking Customer
### Profile
Online or omnichannel shopper looking for help building a polished outfit for a specific occasion or wardrobe need.

### Needs
- Confidence that products work together.
- Inspiration that feels personal, not random.
- Clear recommendations for the next item to add.

### Pain Points
- Unsure what matches a core garment.
- Overwhelmed by too many disconnected choices.
- Lacks time or expertise to style a complete look.

### Platform Implications
- Recommendations must be easy to understand and visually coherent.
- Explanations should be short and confidence-building.
- Occasion and context cues should influence ranking and copy.

## Persona 2: Returning Customer With Known Preferences
### Profile
Customer with purchase history, browsing history, and emerging style patterns.

### Needs
- Personalized suggestions that complement prior purchases.
- Seasonal wardrobe progression.
- Relevant reminders and prompts for style gaps.

### Pain Points
- Sees repetitive recommendations.
- Has to rediscover style preferences every season.

### Platform Implications
- Style profile and identity resolution are critical.
- Recommendation logic should distinguish between novelty, complement, and replacement.

## Persona 3: In-Store Sales Associate / Client Advisor
### Profile
Store associate supporting RTW and CM customers during appointments and follow-up selling.

### Needs
- Quick access to complete-look suggestions.
- Context-aware recommendations tied to appointment purpose and customer history.
- Confidence that suggestions align with stock, style, and tailoring context.

### Pain Points
- Styling knowledge is not always system-supported.
- Follow-up selling can be inconsistent after appointments.

### Platform Implications
- Clienteling surfaces must be fast, explainable, and editable.
- CM workflows need complementary item logic beyond stock-on-hand garments.

## Persona 4: Merchandising Manager
### Profile
Business owner responsible for style direction, curation, and recommendation governance.

### Needs
- Ability to define curated looks and business rules.
- Visibility into how AI recommendations are assembled and performing.
- Approval gates before high-visibility recommendations go live.

### Pain Points
- Current systems do not fully operationalize merchandising intent.
- Recommendation tooling often reduces control over presentation.

### Platform Implications
- Admin tools need clear override, approval, and audit patterns.
- Rule builder and look editor are first-class capabilities.

## Persona 5: CRM / Email Marketing Manager
### Profile
Owner of lifecycle campaigns, triggered messages, and personalized content blocks.

### Needs
- Recommendation payloads suitable for campaign assembly.
- Ability to target by occasion, region, and customer profile.
- Confidence that recommended looks are brand-consistent and available.

### Pain Points
- Campaign personalization is often disconnected from merchandising curation.
- Recommendation freshness and availability can be hard to trust.

### Platform Implications
- Delivery API should support channel formatting and campaign constraints.
- Recommendation campaigns need preview, approval, and experiment support.

## Persona 6: Product Manager / Product Architect
### Profile
Owner of roadmap, delivery sequencing, and cross-functional decision-making.

### Needs
- Clear feature decomposition.
- Stage-gated workflow from idea to QA.
- Shared context docs to reduce ambiguity across agents and humans.

### Pain Points
- Early ambiguity creates downstream rework.
- Planning documents often do not stay connected to execution.

### Platform Implications
- Boards and artifact standards must be explicit.
- Every feature should have traceability across requirement, design, build, and QA.
