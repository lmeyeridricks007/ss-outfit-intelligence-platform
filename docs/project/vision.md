# Vision

## Purpose
State why the AI Outfit Intelligence Platform should exist, what long-term product position it should achieve, and how it should differentiate SuitSupply.

## Practical usage
Use this document to align roadmap, architecture, and downstream feature prioritization to the same product ambition.

## Product vision
Build the recommendation platform that helps SuitSupply customers assemble complete, context-aware outfits with the same confidence and taste they would expect from the brand's best in-store styling experience.

The platform should move SuitSupply from isolated product recommendations toward guided complete-look discovery across ecommerce, clienteling, and marketing channels. Recommendations should feel curated, relevant, and commercially sound rather than generic or purely co-occurrence-driven.

## Long-term ambition
The long-term platform goal is to become the shared recommendation and styling decision layer for:
- ecommerce product detail pages, cart, and personalized homepage experiences
- style inspiration and look builder journeys
- marketing activation such as personalized email and lifecycle campaigns
- in-store stylist and clienteling workflows
- future mobile and API-driven experiences
- both RTW and CM journeys

Over time, the platform should support a closed loop where curated looks, merchandising strategy, customer behavior, inventory, and contextual signals continuously improve recommendation quality.

## Why this product should exist
SuitSupply already has strong brand taste, styling knowledge, and merchandising expertise. That knowledge is valuable, but it is difficult to activate consistently at scale across channels without a shared recommendation foundation. Customers are asking style-completion questions that typical ecommerce recommendation systems are poorly suited to answer.

The platform should exist to:
- help customers complete outfits instead of abandoning styling decisions
- turn brand styling expertise into a reusable digital capability
- improve recommendation relevance while preserving merchandising control
- connect customer profile, context, and product compatibility into one decision system

## Differentiators
The product should differentiate SuitSupply by combining:
- complete-look recommendation logic instead of isolated item similarity
- curated looks, rule-based compatibility, and AI ranking in one governed stack
- support for occasion-aware and weather-aware recommendations
- cross-channel personalization rather than channel-specific silos
- explicit support for RTW and CM journeys
- strong operator control through merchandising rules, campaigns, and auditability

## Intended impact
If executed well, the platform should:
- increase conversion and basket size by making outfit completion easier
- improve customer trust in recommendations because outputs feel stylistically coherent
- give stylists and merchandisers stronger digital leverage across channels
- create a durable data and experimentation foundation for recommendation optimization

## Vision principles
1. Complete look before isolated item.
2. Curated taste and AI ranking must work together.
3. Context matters: season, weather, location, and occasion should influence outcomes.
4. Personalization must be useful, explainable, and privacy-aware.
5. Recommendations must remain operationally valid with inventory, catalog, and governance controls.
6. The platform should be API-first so every channel can consume the same core intelligence.
