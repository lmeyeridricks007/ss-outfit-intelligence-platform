# AI Outfit Intelligence Platform Goals

## Goal Framework
This document defines the success conditions that all project artifacts, design choices, and implementation plans should trace back to.

## Business Goals
- Increase conversion rate on recommendation-enabled surfaces.
- Increase average order value through complete-look cross-sell and upsell.
- Improve repeat purchase and customer lifetime value through personalized style progression.
- Increase engagement with editorial and inspirational shopping journeys.
- Improve productivity of merchandising and CRM teams through reusable recommendation assets and governed automation.

## Customer Goals
- Reduce uncertainty about what matches, what fits the occasion, and what to buy next.
- Make it easier to build a complete outfit instead of shopping item by item.
- Provide personalized inspiration without feeling random or overly promotional.
- Support both self-service online journeys and assisted in-store styling.

## Product Goals
- Serve complete-look recommendations across webstore, PDP, cart, homepage, email, and clienteling tools.
- Support recommendation types including outfit recommendations, cross-sell, upsell, style bundles, occasion-based suggestions, contextual recommendations, and personal recommendations.
- Enable mixing of curated, rule-based, and AI-ranked outputs.
- Allow merchandising teams to define, edit, approve, and monitor recommendation logic.
- Expose recommendation delivery through stable APIs and widget contracts.

## Operational Goals
- Standardize feature delivery from idea through QA using GitHub, Cursor Cloud Agents, checklists, and approval gates.
- Make every delivery stage auditable through markdown artifacts and board state transitions.
- Create enough structure that future agents can generate documents, prompts, templates, plans, and implementation assets without restating project context.
- Separate draft, review, and approval states clearly to reduce premature implementation.

## Data and Intelligence Goals
- Create durable customer style profiles using behavioral, transactional, and contextual data.
- Build a product and outfit graph that models compatibility, substitution, and occasion relevance.
- Capture performance telemetry needed for A/B testing, attribution, and optimization.
- Preserve data lineage, quality controls, and explainability for recommendation logic.

## Governance Goals
- Ensure AI outputs can be reviewed before business-critical publication.
- Define clear ownership between product, merchandising, engineering, data, and review roles.
- Keep privacy, consent, identity resolution, and customer trust central to the design.

## Non-Goals
- Building a production model training pipeline in the first iteration.
- Replacing core commerce systems such as catalog, order management, or CRM.
- Providing fully autonomous publishing without human approval gates.
- Solving general fashion search beyond recommendation and styling use cases.

## Example Success Metrics
- Recommendation-driven revenue uplift.
- Attach rate for outfit components.
- Click-through rate on recommendation widgets by surface.
- Conversion rate lift for recommendation-enabled PDP and cart experiences.
- Email engagement lift for personalized look blocks.
- Associate adoption in clienteling workflows.
- Merchandising rule coverage and override usage.
- Experiment velocity and time from feature request to approved implementation.
