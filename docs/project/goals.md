# Goals

## Business goals

The platform should improve commercial performance by making recommendations more complete, more relevant, and more actionable across channels.

Primary business goals:

- increase ecommerce conversion through better style guidance
- increase average order value by driving outfit completion across categories
- increase customer lifetime value through more relevant personalization
- improve performance of clienteling and email recommendation programs
- create a reusable recommendation foundation instead of separate channel-specific logic

## User goals

The product should help customers and internal teams make better outfit decisions with less effort.

Customer goals:

- discover complete outfits that work together
- shop confidently for specific occasions, seasons, and locations
- receive recommendations that reflect personal style and purchase history
- understand what to add next when viewing a product or configuring a garment

Internal team goals:

- give stylists faster access to relevant outfit guidance
- let merchandisers influence recommendation outcomes through curated looks and business rules
- let marketing teams activate recommendation sets in campaigns without bespoke logic
- give product and analytics teams measurable recommendation performance data

## Operational goals

The platform should be operationally reliable, governable, and extensible.

Operational goals:

- centralize recommendation logic behind shared services and standards
- support identity resolution and profile enrichment across channels
- preserve auditability of which curated, rule-based, and AI-ranked factors shaped a recommendation set
- support experimentation and controlled rollout by channel and use case
- allow phased rollout starting with high-value surfaces such as PDP and cart

## Success criteria

The product is successful when it demonstrates measurable value and produces a sustainable operating model.

### Outcome metrics

- conversion rate uplift in the target range of +5% to +10%
- average order value uplift in the target range of +10% to +25%
- increased attach rates for complementary categories
- stronger repeat purchase and engagement behavior for identified customer segments
- higher recommendation engagement in email and clienteling workflows

### Product health metrics

- recommendation coverage across key categories and surfaces
- recommendation click-through, add-to-cart, save, and purchase rates
- recommendation acceptance rate for curated and AI-ranked outfit suggestions
- latency and availability for recommendation delivery APIs
- override rate and rule conflict rate for merchandising operations

### Operational readiness metrics

- percentage of recommendation responses traceable to source inputs and rule context
- time required for merchandising teams to launch or adjust curated looks and campaigns
- percentage of major channels using the shared recommendation platform

## Non-goals

The initial project documentation and early platform releases should not assume the following:

- full autonomous styling without merchandising control
- replacement of core commerce, POS, or marketing systems
- generation of final garment imagery or creative assets for every recommendation
- full wardrobe planning or subscription styling outside the defined recommendation surfaces
- universal rollout to every channel on day one
- immediate use of every possible data source before foundational ingestion and identity work are stable
