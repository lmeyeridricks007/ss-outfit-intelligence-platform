# Vision

## Product vision

Build the category-leading AI Outfit Intelligence Platform for SuitSupply: a system that recommends complete, context-aware outfits across Ready-to-Wear (RTW) and Custom Made (CM), instead of only suggesting similar individual products.

The platform should turn SuitSupply's existing styling expertise into a scalable digital capability that helps customers answer practical wardrobe questions with confidence:

- What completes this look?
- What is appropriate for this occasion?
- What works in this season and location?
- What reflects my style, history, and budget?

## Why this product should exist

SuitSupply already has strong merchandising, styling, and product knowledge, but customers still experience the catalog as a set of separate items instead of a coordinated wardrobe system. That gap creates friction for shoppers, missed cross-category demand, and inconsistent recommendation quality across ecommerce, email, and clienteling channels.

This product should exist to:

- help customers move from single-item shopping to outfit-based decision making
- convert internal styling knowledge into repeatable, data-driven recommendation logic
- give merchandising teams controlled leverage over AI-driven personalization
- create a reusable recommendation platform for every customer touchpoint

## Long-term ambition

Over time, the platform should become the decisioning layer behind all personalized style guidance at SuitSupply. It should support:

- customer-facing outfit recommendations across web, email, mobile, and future channels
- stylist-assisted recommendations in stores and clienteling workflows
- merchandising controls for curated looks, rules, campaigns, and seasonal priorities
- optimization loops that learn from customer behavior, purchases, inventory, and context

The long-term ambition is not only to recommend products, but to help customers build wardrobes that feel intentional, relevant, and distinctly SuitSupply.

## Differentiators

The platform is differentiated by combining multiple recommendation sources instead of relying on co-occurrence alone:

- curated looks created or approved by merchandising and styling teams
- compatibility rules for category, color, fabric, fit, formality, and occasion
- AI ranking that personalizes recommendations using customer behavior and profile signals
- context-aware adjustments based on season, geography, weather, and occasion
- support for both RTW and CM recommendation logic

## Intended market and user impact

For customers, the product should reduce decision fatigue and make high-consideration apparel purchases easier. For SuitSupply teams, it should create a consistent recommendation capability that can be activated across commerce, marketing, and clienteling surfaces.

Expected impact includes:

- higher conversion from product discovery to purchase
- larger baskets through cross-category outfit completion
- stronger repeat engagement from more relevant personalization
- better activation of style expertise at scale

## Vision principles

The platform should be guided by the following principles:

1. Recommend complete outfits, not only adjacent products.
2. Keep merchandising control visible even when AI ranking is used.
3. Use context and customer signals when they improve relevance.
4. Treat RTW and CM as related but distinct recommendation problems.
5. Make recommendations deployable across multiple channels through shared services and standards.
