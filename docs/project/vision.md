# Vision

## Product vision
AI Outfit Intelligence Platform is SuitSupply's decisioning and delivery layer for complete-look recommendations across digital and in-store channels. The platform should help customers move from choosing a single product to confidently building a full outfit that fits their occasion, personal style, climate, and budget.

## Long-term product ambition
The long-term ambition is to make complete-look recommendation a core capability of SuitSupply's commerce and clienteling stack so every channel can:
- propose coherent outfits instead of isolated products
- combine expert curation with AI ranking and customer context
- adapt recommendations for Ready-to-Wear (RTW) and Custom Made (CM)
- learn from customer behavior, merchandiser input, and business outcomes over time

## Why this product should exist
SuitSupply already has strong brand taste, product expertise, and merchandising knowledge, but customers still need help translating that expertise into purchase decisions. A dedicated outfit intelligence platform should exist because:
- customers shop for outcomes such as weddings, work, travel, and seasonal refreshes rather than for isolated SKUs
- styling confidence is a major conversion barrier in tailoring and occasionwear
- manual curation alone does not scale consistently across all channels, markets, and sessions
- generic recommender systems are too narrow because they over-index on similarity and co-occurrence instead of style compatibility and context

## Differentiators
This product should differentiate from standard ecommerce recommenders in four ways:
1. **Complete-look orientation:** recommendations optimize for a coherent outfit, not just the next likely item.
2. **Hybrid intelligence:** the platform combines curated looks, compatibility rules, business controls, and AI ranking.
3. **Context awareness:** outputs reflect occasion, weather, season, geography, inventory, and session intent.
4. **Omnichannel activation:** the same recommendation logic can power PDP, cart, homepage, email, and clienteling surfaces.

## Intended market and user impact
For customers, the platform should reduce styling uncertainty and make SuitSupply feel more like a trusted advisor than a catalog. For internal teams, it should turn styling knowledge into reusable, measurable recommendation assets that can be deployed consistently across channels.

## Strategic product position
The platform is not only a recommendation widget. It is a product capability that sits between product data, customer understanding, merchandising intent, and channel delivery. If successful, it becomes the foundation for:
- personalized style guidance across owned channels
- smarter cross-sell and upsell motions
- better support for stylists and clienteling teams
- more measurable merchandising and lifecycle marketing programs
