# Problem Statement

## Current problem

SuitSupply customers often shop from a single product entry point, such as a suit, jacket, shirt, or accessory, but need help assembling a full outfit. Existing ecommerce recommendation approaches usually optimize for item similarity, co-purchase frequency, or popularity. Those methods are useful for product discovery, but they do not reliably answer complete-look questions.

As a result, customers still have to infer:

- what shirt, tie, shoes, belt, or pocket square fits a selected suit
- what outfit works for a specific occasion such as a wedding, interview, or business meeting
- what products make sense for the current season, weather, and location
- how prior purchases or known style preferences should influence the next recommendation

## Who experiences the problem

### Primary affected users

- online shoppers building outfits across multiple categories
- returning customers whose prior purchases should influence recommendations
- occasion-driven customers who need style guidance, not just product suggestions

### Secondary affected users

- in-store stylists who need efficient complete-look suggestions
- merchandisers who want to scale curated style guidance across channels
- marketers who need recommendation content for lifecycle campaigns
- product and analytics teams who need measurable recommendation performance

## Why current alternatives are insufficient

Current alternatives are insufficient because they typically focus on:

- similar products within the same category
- frequently bought together relationships without style reasoning
- popularity signals that ignore individual customer context
- limited or inconsistent use of weather, season, occasion, and geography
- siloed logic by channel rather than a shared omnichannel recommendation platform

These approaches fail when customers need a stylistic answer instead of a statistical association. They also underuse SuitSupply's internal curation and merchandising expertise.

## Why now

This problem is timely because:

- SuitSupply has enough product breadth to benefit from outfit-level cross-category recommendations
- customer expectations increasingly favor personalized guidance, not just product lists
- email, web personalization, and clienteling programs need a common recommendation layer
- AI ranking can now be productively combined with rules, curated looks, and customer signals
- the business impact is material, with explicit targets for conversion and basket size improvement

## Consequence of not solving it

If the problem remains unsolved:

- customers will continue to receive fragmented recommendations that do not confidently complete an outfit
- cross-sell potential across shirts, ties, shoes, belts, outerwear, and accessories will remain under-realized
- marketing and clienteling teams will continue to rely on manual curation or channel-specific logic
- merchandising knowledge will remain difficult to scale consistently
- experimentation and optimization will be limited by inconsistent recommendation definitions and telemetry
- competitors with stronger complete-look guidance may create a more helpful shopping experience

## Core product challenge

The platform must convert merchandising taste, compatibility logic, customer behavior, and live context into recommendations that are:

- stylistically coherent
- commercially useful
- channel-ready
- measurable
- governable
- safe to operate across regions and customer data constraints
