# Goals

## Business goals

- Increase conversion rate by making recommendations more relevant and actionable at key decision points.
- Increase basket size and average order value by recommending complementary items that complete a look.
- Improve customer lifetime value by making recommendations more personal and more useful across repeat visits and repeat purchases.
- Improve campaign and clienteling effectiveness by making recommendation outputs reusable across ecommerce, email, and in-store workflows.
- Preserve and scale SuitSupply merchandising expertise through governed recommendation logic rather than ad hoc manual curation.

## User goals

### Customer goals
- Build complete outfits, not just purchase single products.
- Get recommendations that match a specific occasion, season, and local context.
- Discover compatible complementary items with less guesswork.
- Receive recommendations that feel aligned with personal taste, fit preferences, and prior purchases.
- See suggestions that are actually purchasable and relevant to the current journey.

### Internal user goals
- Give merchandisers a way to curate looks, define overrides, and protect brand standards.
- Give stylists and clienteling teams reusable recommendation outputs they can trust in customer conversations.
- Give marketing teams recommendation content that can be inserted into lifecycle and campaign messages.
- Give analytics and optimization teams measurable outputs, experimentation hooks, and performance feedback loops.

## Operational goals

- Establish a canonical recommendation delivery API that serves multiple channels consistently.
- Build a reusable data foundation for customer signals, context, product attributes, and recommendation telemetry.
- Support both RTW and CM recommendation logic without forcing them into one identical workflow.
- Separate curated, rule-based, and AI-ranked recommendation sources so governance and optimization are explicit.
- Ensure recommendation generation is auditable, observable, and safe to operate across regions and consent boundaries.

## Success criteria

### Business outcomes
- Conversion uplift in the range of 5% to 10% on targeted surfaces.
- Average order value uplift in the range of 10% to 25% for recommendation-influenced sessions.
- Improved repeat purchase and engagement behavior for customers exposed to personalized outfit recommendations.
- Stronger performance in recommendation-enabled email and clienteling workflows.

### Product outcomes
- Complete-look recommendation coverage on primary digital surfaces.
- Multi-type recommendation support for outfit, cross-sell, upsell, style bundle, occasion-based, contextual, and personal recommendations.
- Reusable recommendation outputs available for ecommerce, marketing, and in-store consumers.
- Merchandising controls that can override or shape recommendation behavior without engineering intervention for every change.

### Operational outcomes
- Reliable ingestion of product, event, and customer signals required for recommendation generation.
- Traceable recommendation telemetry for impression, click, save, add-to-cart, purchase, dismiss, and override events.
- Experimentation support for ranking strategies, rule sets, and surface presentation.

## Non-goals

- Building a generic marketplace recommendation platform for brands outside SuitSupply.
- Replacing core commerce, POS, OMS, or ESP systems.
- Solving warehouse allocation, demand forecasting, or full assortment planning in this bootstrap scope.
- Providing fully autonomous styling without merchandising rules, human governance, or brand control.
- Delivering every downstream feature or workflow in the first release phase.

## Guiding principles

- Optimize for complete-look usefulness, not recommendation volume.
- Keep business control and measurement first-class, not afterthoughts.
- Prefer explainable recommendation composition and ranking over opaque outputs.
- Design for phased rollout across surfaces and regions rather than one large launch.
