# Goals

## Business goals

- Increase conversion by reducing the friction of building a complete outfit.
- Increase average order value by improving attach rates across shirts, ties, shoes, belts, pocket squares, outerwear, and other complementary categories.
- Increase repeat purchase behavior and customer lifetime value through more relevant personalization and wardrobe extension recommendations.
- Improve the performance of recommendation-driven email, clienteling, and web personalization programs by reusing shared recommendation logic.
- Preserve and scale merchandising influence while enabling AI-driven ranking, experimentation, and operational learning.

## User goals

- Help shoppers understand what to wear with a viewed, configured, or previously purchased item.
- Help customers build complete looks for occasions such as weddings, business meetings, interviews, travel, and seasonal wardrobe updates.
- Reduce the time and uncertainty involved in pairing products across categories.
- Reflect the customer's personal style, purchase history, browsing behavior, and current context when permitted and reliable.
- Support both self-service shopping and assisted selling without forcing different recommendation logic per channel.

## Operational goals

- Establish a reusable recommendation platform rather than isolated widgets or campaign-specific logic.
- Ingest and normalize product, customer, event, and context signals into a governed recommendation stack.
- Provide a delivery API that can serve multiple channels with stable contracts and surface-aware behavior.
- Give merchandisers and operators the ability to manage curated looks, compatibility rules, exclusions, overrides, and campaign priorities.
- Instrument recommendation impressions and downstream actions so the platform can be measured, audited, and improved.
- Support incremental rollout, experimentation, and safe fallback behavior as the platform expands.

## Success criteria

### Outcome targets

- Conversion rate improvement of 5% to 10% on recommendation-enabled journeys.
- Average order value improvement of 10% to 25% where complete-look recommendations are surfaced.
- Higher engagement with recommendation modules, look-builder flows, and recommendation-driven campaigns.
- Stronger repeat purchase behavior among returning customers exposed to personalized recommendation strategies.

### Product and platform success signals

- Recommendation coverage across priority categories and launch surfaces.
- Improved attach rate for complementary categories on recommendation-enabled journeys.
- Stable response latency suitable for interactive web and assisted-selling experiences.
- Measurable experimental lift between baseline, curated, and AI-ranked recommendation strategies.
- Merchandiser adoption of look curation, rules, and campaign controls.
- Clear traceability from recommendation set generation to clicks, add-to-cart actions, purchases, dismissals, and overrides.

## Non-goals

- Replacing all human styling judgment with fully autonomous AI.
- Launching every channel, region, and recommendation type in the first production release.
- Building a standalone editorial, social, or community content platform.
- Solving inventory planning, pricing optimization, or unrelated merchandising systems in this bootstrap scope.
- Treating RTW and CM as identical workflows when their constraints, data, and decision logic differ.
