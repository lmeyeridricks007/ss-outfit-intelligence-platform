# Problem Statement

## Current problem

SuitSupply customers often know the product category they are browsing but not the full outfit they should buy. A shopper may land on a suit, jacket, shirt, or CM configuration and still need help answering practical style questions:

- what shirt matches this suit
- which tie works with this jacket
- what to wear for a specific occasion
- how to build a full outfit across categories
- how recommendations should reflect personal style and prior purchases

Current ecommerce recommendation approaches typically emphasize similar products, co-purchase patterns, or popularity. Those approaches are useful for narrow substitution and add-on use cases, but they are not reliable for complete outfit building.

## Who experiences the problem

### Primary users

- online shoppers browsing RTW categories
- returning customers with purchase history and style signals
- customers shopping for occasions such as weddings, business meetings, interviews, travel, and seasonal wardrobe refreshes

### Secondary users

- in-store stylists and clienteling teams
- merchandisers curating brand-consistent looks and rules
- marketing teams building personalized recommendation campaigns
- product, analytics, and optimization teams responsible for commercial performance

## Why current alternatives are insufficient

Existing recommendation patterns fail because they usually do not combine the inputs that matter for outfit intelligence:

- style compatibility between categories
- occasion suitability
- seasonality and weather relevance
- customer profile and prior wardrobe signals
- curated merchandising knowledge
- cross-channel identity and behavior

Without these inputs, recommendations drift toward item adjacency rather than outfit coherence. That limits both user trust and commercial value.

## Why now

SuitSupply already has strong internal styling and merchandising expertise, plus multiple customer channels that can benefit from better recommendation decisioning. The missing piece is a platform that operationalizes this knowledge with data, personalization, and reusable delivery across surfaces. Building that platform now creates leverage across ecommerce, email, clienteling, and future personalization surfaces instead of solving each channel independently.

## Consequences of not solving it

If this problem remains unsolved:

- customers continue to do the work of styling for themselves
- recommendation surfaces remain weaker than their channel potential
- attach rates and basket expansion opportunities are missed
- personalization remains inconsistent across channels
- merchandising knowledge stays difficult to scale and measure
- SuitSupply risks looking interchangeable with competitors that only offer similarity-based recommendations

## Desired future state

SuitSupply should be able to generate complete, brand-consistent recommendations that combine AI ranking, curated looks, business rules, inventory reality, and customer context, then deliver those recommendations consistently wherever the customer engages.
