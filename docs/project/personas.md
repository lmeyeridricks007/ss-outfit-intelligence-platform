# Personas

## Purpose
Define the primary and secondary personas that shape product behavior, workflow design, and governance needs.

## Practical usage
Use these personas when prioritizing recommendation journeys, admin tooling, campaign workflows, and analytics.

## Primary personas

### Persona P1: Anchor-product shopper
- **Who they are:** A customer browsing a suit, jacket, shirt, or other anchor product on ecommerce.
- **Goal:** Understand what other items complete the outfit and make a confident purchase decision quickly.
- **Pain points:** Uncertainty about matching categories, fear of making poor style choices, too many isolated product suggestions, limited confidence in generic recommendation widgets.
- **Behavior:** Starts from a specific product, compares options visually, may add one item to cart before deciding on complementary pieces.
- **Decision context:** Often buying for work, an event, or wardrobe refresh with limited time for styling research.
- **Success looks like:** The customer sees a coherent outfit recommendation, trusts it, and adds multiple complementary items.

### Persona P2: Returning style-profile customer
- **Who they are:** A repeat customer with past orders, browsing history, and style signals.
- **Goal:** Receive recommendations that reflect prior purchases, preferred fits, color tendencies, and spending patterns.
- **Pain points:** Recommendations that ignore prior wardrobe context, duplicate owned items, or suggest irrelevant styles.
- **Behavior:** Returns through direct traffic, email, or logged-in sessions; may browse multiple categories before purchase.
- **Decision context:** Expects the brand to remember preferences and reduce decision effort.
- **Success looks like:** Recommendations feel personalized, complementary to prior purchases, and appropriate for current season or occasion.

### Persona P3: Occasion-led shopper
- **Who they are:** A customer shopping for a wedding, interview, business meeting, travel wardrobe, or seasonal update.
- **Goal:** Build a suitable outfit for a specific setting rather than starting from a single product.
- **Pain points:** Difficulty translating occasion intent into a complete outfit, uncertainty about climate or formality appropriateness.
- **Behavior:** May browse style inspiration or search-led pages before landing on products.
- **Decision context:** Occasion urgency is high, and mistakes feel costly.
- **Success looks like:** The customer receives complete-look recommendations that clearly map to the occasion and environment.

## Secondary personas

### Persona S1: In-store stylist or clienteling associate
- **Goal:** Assemble relevant looks faster while preserving premium service quality.
- **Pain points:** Manual outfit composition takes time, customer context is fragmented, and digital channels may not reflect in-store styling logic.
- **Behavior:** Uses clienteling workflows during appointments, follow-up outreach, or assisted selling.
- **Success looks like:** The associate can retrieve, tailor, and explain recommendations with minimal friction.

### Persona S2: Merchandiser
- **Goal:** Shape recommendation outputs to reflect brand standards, campaigns, seasonal priorities, and assortment constraints.
- **Pain points:** Limited operator control, slow engineering dependency for changes, weak visibility into recommendation reasoning.
- **Behavior:** Creates curated looks, sets merchandising rules, reviews campaign performance, and manages exceptions.
- **Success looks like:** Merchandising can govern recommendation behavior through auditable controls and clear precedence rules.

### Persona S3: Marketing manager
- **Goal:** Deliver personalized recommendation content in email and lifecycle campaigns.
- **Pain points:** Channel-specific logic, weak personalization quality, poor traceability between recommendation exposure and outcomes.
- **Behavior:** Builds audience segments and campaigns tied to seasonal, behavioral, or occasion triggers.
- **Success looks like:** Recommendation content is reusable, measurable, and aligned with customer profile and context.

### Persona S4: Product, analytics, and optimization team member
- **Goal:** Measure recommendation quality, run experiments, and improve business outcomes safely.
- **Pain points:** Inconsistent telemetry, weak attribution, no shared taxonomy for recommendation types or surfaces.
- **Behavior:** Reviews dashboards, experiments, funnel impacts, and operator behavior.
- **Success looks like:** Recommendation performance is measurable, explainable, and improvable across channels.

## Persona implications for the product
- Recommendation journeys must support both anchor-product and occasion-led entry points.
- Personalization should complement, not replace, curated and rule-based outfit logic.
- Internal users require governance, override controls, and traceability, not just customer-facing widgets.
- The platform must support both self-service digital experiences and assisted-selling workflows.
