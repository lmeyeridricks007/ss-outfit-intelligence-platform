# Goals

## Business goals

- Increase ecommerce conversion by making recommendations more actionable and outfit-oriented.
- Increase basket size and average order value through cross-category outfit building and premium upsell opportunities.
- Improve customer lifetime value by making recommendations more relevant to individual customers and repeat shopping patterns.
- Extend SuitSupply's merchandising expertise into scalable digital and in-store recommendation workflows.
- Improve performance of personalized email, clienteling, and on-site recommendation surfaces.

## User goals

- Help customers understand what goes well together for a specific product, occasion, season, or location.
- Reduce uncertainty when building a full outfit across suits, shirts, shoes, belts, ties, pocket squares, outerwear, and CM options.
- Make recommendations feel personalized without requiring the customer to manually describe every preference.
- Allow shoppers to move from inspiration to purchase with fewer steps and less guesswork.
- Give stylists and merchandisers confidence that recommendations reflect brand standards and current assortment realities.

## Operational goals

- Establish a reusable recommendation platform instead of isolated recommendation logic in each channel.
- Centralize customer profile, context, catalog, compatibility, and curated-look inputs into one delivery model.
- Support controlled experimentation, analytics, and governance for recommendation quality.
- Allow merchandisers to influence recommendation behavior through rules, curated looks, and overrides.
- Maintain a clear boundary between recommendation decisioning, channel presentation, and source-system ownership.

## Success criteria

### Commercial success

- Conversion uplift in the target range of 5% to 10% on recommendation-enabled surfaces.
- Average order value uplift in the target range of 10% to 25% for relevant cohorts and journeys.
- Higher attach rates for complementary items in complete-look and cross-sell journeys.
- Stronger repeat purchase and engagement rates in personalized channels such as email and clienteling.

### Product success

- Customers can receive outfit, cross-sell, upsell, occasion-based, contextual, and personal recommendations from a common platform.
- Major recommendation surfaces are served by a shared delivery API and common business logic.
- RTW and CM recommendation flows are both supported with explicit differences where needed.
- Merchandising and experimentation teams can control, inspect, and measure recommendation behavior.

### Operational success

- Recommendation events are traceable from impression through click, cart, and purchase.
- Recommendation quality can be monitored by channel, cohort, context, and model or rule source.
- Source data quality, identity resolution confidence, and inventory constraints are visible enough to support reliable operations.

## Non-goals

- Replacing core commerce, OMS, POS, email, or analytics systems of record.
- Delivering a final production-grade machine learning model specification in this bootstrap phase.
- Designing every downstream feature, issue, workflow detail, or UI artifact at bootstrap time.
- Recommending products that ignore stock, channel eligibility, consent, or merchandising controls.
- Removing human merchandising oversight from recommendation experiences.
